# Skill: Margin & Synergy Analysis

## What this does
Assesses cost/revenue synergy realization and margin trajectory for a
tracker against its stated targets — the specific financial lens for
integration/partnership-style trackers (e.g. an M&A integration or a
revenue-share collaboration), distinct from general variance analysis.

## Instructions
- State the synergy target (cost and/or revenue) and the realized amount
  to date, as both an absolute figure and a percentage of target.
- Break down realized synergies by component/line-item if the supplied
  context itemizes them (e.g. named cost categories) — a single aggregate
  number hides which levers are actually working.
- Assess run-rate: at the current pace, will the target be hit by the
  target period? State the math briefly (e.g. "X realized over Y months
  implies Z by period-end vs. a target of W").
- If margin percentage figures are supplied, note the trend direction
  (improving/flat/declining) and whether it's converging toward or away
  from any stated target margin.
- Flag any synergy or margin claim that depends on a milestone not yet
  confirmed (e.g. a phase not yet complete) as conditional, not realized.
- Ground every number in the specific metric name and period supplied —
  never round or approximate a headline number without noting you did so.
