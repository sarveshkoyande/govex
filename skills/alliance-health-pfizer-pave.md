# Skill: Alliance Health Framework — Pfizer PAVE Collaboration

## What this does
Applies an alliance-management/relationship-health framework to the Pfizer
PAVE Collaboration tracker specifically. PAVE is an ONGOING performance-
based commercialization partnership (Oct 2025 – Dec 2028), not a pre-deal
sourcing decision and not an acquisition — so pre-deal frameworks (Vested
Outsourcing, deal-structuring checklists) don't fit, and integration
frameworks (7S) don't fit either, since nothing is being merged. The right
lens for an ongoing alliance is: is the partnership itself staying healthy
across its life — strategically aligned, well-governed, delivering the
shared economics it was built around, and resilient to the frictions that
kill alliances long after signing. Do not apply this skill to trackers that
are not live, ongoing partnership/alliance structures (e.g. BioPharm & Media
is a post-merger integration — use framework-7s-biopharm.md for that).

## The six dimensions, grounded in the actual PAVE agreement

| Dimension | What "healthy" looks like for PAVE | Tracking signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Strategic Alignment** | Indegene's activity is genuinely converting mature, established Pfizer brands (Besponsa, Bosulif, Inlyta, Mylotarg, Xalkori, Duavee, Estring, Premarin, Prempro, Eucrisa, Somavert) into incremental gross margin above the Binding Baseline Amount — the core economic premise still holds. | Progress against the Target Incremental Amounts: $17.17M (2026), $31.18M (2027), $29.33M (2028). |
| **Governance Cadence** | The Joint Steering Committee (Pfizer-chaired, monthly) and its subcommittees (Operational Governance weekly, AdPromo, Partnership Review, AI, Forecasting & Supply Chain, Training & Transition) are actually meeting and making decisions — not decaying into a rubber stamp or, worse, silence. | Whether JSC/OGC cadence commitments are being met; whether PDE targets, baseline updates, and product add/remove decisions are flowing through the JSC as designed rather than around it. |
| **Performance & Financial Health** | The Gross Margin Share mechanics are tracking as modeled — Indegene earning its 89%/67% (2026) or 67%/28% (2027-28) share bands, the quarterly OpEx floor ($12M/$10M/$9M) covering operations without a Q4 true-up shock, and PDE volume genuinely proxying real detailing impact via the DRE model. | Actual vs. forecasted Gross Margin Percentage (90% target for 2026); PDE volume trend; whether Q1-Q3 payments plus Q4 true-up are landing close to the annual Gross Margin Share estimate (a large true-up swing signals the forecasting/baseline process itself is unhealthy). |
| **Relationship & Trust** | Alliance Managers on both sides are functioning as real day-to-day points of contact, not just named titles; escalations are resolving at the JSC level, not routinely climbing to Executive Officers (Aamir Malik / Gaurav Kapoor) or the Independent Expert. | Frequency and severity of items escalated past the JSC; whether the same relationship issues keep recurring across governance cycles. |
| **Risk & Compliance (esp. AI)** | The dedicated AI Committee is exercising real oversight of AI-enabled campaign operations (Agentforce, Invisage™, the AWS data lake) — Pfizer's evident compliance sensitivity here (a whole subcommittee for it) means gaps here are higher-severity than ordinary delivery risk. Per the context doc: AI segmentation is NOT broadly implemented — limited to specific use cases like Invisage — so any claim of portfolio-wide AI capability is a red flag, not a strength, and should be corrected rather than let stand. | Whether AI Committee reviews are current; whether internal narrative accurately reflects the LIMITED scope of AI segmentation rather than overstating it. |
| **Flexibility & Resilience** | Both sides' exit/adjustment mechanics are understood and unused in anger — Pfizer's 90-day no-cause termination and per-product removal rights, Indegene's 6-month no-cause exit, and the wind-down reimbursement protecting Indegene's committed third-party costs. A partnership that's healthy rarely needs these; frequent invocation is itself the signal. | Any product removal, baseline dispute, or wind-down-fee conversation — these are lagging indicators the relationship has already strained. |

## Known context nuances — treat these as established, not open questions
- **Premarin's baseline is not "unresolved."** It can't be adjusted yet purely because of a SOW-driven timing constraint (adjustment is only permitted after two subsequent quarters). Frame this as a scheduled, timing-dependent adjustment — never as an unresolved risk or an open question requiring escalation.
- **Premarin is ~8-10% of total portfolio.** Don't let it dominate a strategic narrative or a risk narrative disproportionate to its actual weight.
- **AI segmentation is narrow, not broad.** Limited to specific use cases (Invisage is the named one). Never generalize AI capability across the whole PAVE portfolio — that overstatement is itself a governance/compliance risk given how seriously this agreement treats AI oversight (see Risk & Compliance above).

## Instructions
- Populate all six dimensions using only what's actually recorded for this tracker — strategy goals, tactics/execution insights, financials (budget/spend/forecast, synergy figures), stakeholders, risks, and raw ingestion events. If a dimension has no real evidence either way, say so explicitly rather than inventing filler.
- For each dimension, state whether it looks HEALTHY, AT RISK, or UNCLEAR, with the specific fact or gap behind that call.
- Apply the "known context nuances" above whenever Premarin or AI capability comes up — do not re-litigate them as open questions.
- Close with a one-sentence "so what": which ONE of the six dimensions is the highest-leverage relationship risk right now, given the balance of all six.
- This is a structured positioning lens, not a replacement for the tracker's own Strategy-vs-Outcome cards — when used during synthesis, let it surface alliance-health-specific framing (e.g. a governance-cadence gap or an AI-oversight nuance) the standard synthesis pass might state in purely execution terms instead.
